Preamble

The Constitution of the Republic of South Africa (Section 27) states that everyone has the right to have access to sufficient food and water.

Food security exists when all people at all times have both physical and economic access to sufficient food to meet their dietary needs for a productive and healthy life. Achieving food security requires that:

  • Sufficient quantities of appropriate foods are consistently available.
  • Individuals have adequate incomes or other resources to purchase or barter for food.
  • Food is properly processed and stored.
  • Individuals have sound knowledge of nutrition and child care that they put to good use, and have access to adequate health and sanitation services.

    • Food security is at the top of the list of Millennium Development Goals (MDGs) with the goal of eradicating poverty and hunger.

The UN Committee on Economic, Social and Cultural Rights considered that the core content of the right to adequate food implies the availability of food in quantity sufficient to satisfy the dietary needs of individuals, free from adverse substances, and acceptable within a given culture. The right to be free from hunger is the minimum core content (minimum essential level) of the right to food.

The accessibility (which encompasses both economic and physical accessibility) of such food should be in ways that are sustainable and that do not interfere with the enjoyment of other human rights.

The Purpose of this Charter

This Charter of Fundamental Agricultural Values sets out a framework for all the stakeholders in Agriculture in South Africa to address the challenge of ensuring the availability of sufficient quantities of appropriate foods to all the citizens of South Africa recognising that:

1. Population growth and

2. Increasing unemployment and

3. Finite productive agricultural land and

4. Increasingly compromised and scarce water resources and the

5. Rising cost of producing affordable food and fibre

calls for agreement on the shared fundamental values that will take sustainable agriculture forward to the benefit of all the stakeholders.

It recognises that no single stakeholder can guarantee the right of everyone to have access to sufficient food and that all stakeholders share that responsibility in equal measure.

Scope of Application

The Charter applies to the benefit of all South Africans.

In the event that a principle set forth is not dischargeable due to a local constraint the value shall be implemented to the extent possible and practicable in pursuit of the overall goal. Continuous attempts should be made to resolve the local constraints.

Primary agriculture is committed to promoting the application of the values of this Charter to all of the stakeholders of agriculture. As such primary agriculture commits to work with all stakeholders to implement to the furthest extent possible these values in their individual areas of jurisdiction or responsibility.

Commitments

A Common Purpose


Unless we can define a common purpose we can all believe in and work towards, we might as well go home.

We are trying to build a community of Purpose-driven stakeholders. And it's only when that community has solid agreement on purposes and principles that we can start talking about the concept and structure of the agriculture.

Commitment #2

We commit ourselves to a process of ongoing improvement in the availability of sufficient quantities of appropriate foods for all the citizens of South Africa. This commitment is our common purpose.


Common Constraints


A constraint is anything that prevents the system from achieving its goal.

Since (by common agreement) we have not reached the relevant MDG Goal we have to conclude that the system is constrained.

Commitment #3

We commit ourselves to identifying, describing and reducing the constraints that limit our ability to have sufficient quantities of appropriate foods for all the citizens of South Africa

A Sense of Urgency


By 2030, more than one in three human beings will not have enough to drink, or will run the risk of dying by drinking what they've got. Today, the prospect of such scarcity is causing countries to mine so-called fossil water from deep aquifers that were formed millions of years ago.

If India gave up groundwater mining, its grain production would likely fall by 25%, leaving it incapable of feeding itself. Nobody knows precisely how long this can continue, but the answer will be measured in decades, not in centuries.[http://www.fastcompany.com/58870/business-30]

Commitment #4

We commit ourselves to taking action on critical issues now; not when it is convenient, expedient or politically correct.

Single View of the Challlenge


Politics is about hierarchy and preserving priviledged positions. Hierarchy is therefore not primarily about communication (which is its responsibility) but to control information.

Commitment #5

We commit ourselves to transparency. Instead of keeping things hidden and away from common scrutiny and concern we open them up, however difficult or painful that might be. Only when we all understand the real issues will we be able to make better choices.
